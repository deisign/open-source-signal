# Source policy

The source registry is split into:

- `data/sources_web.yaml` — websites, blogs, newsletters, datasets and public project pages.
- `data/sources_telegram.yaml` — Telegram channels or candidate Telegram sources.

The registry is editorial infrastructure. A source appearing in the registry does not mean every claim from that source is publication-ready.

## Source roles

Use these source roles:

- `primary` — original publication, official documentation, source material, primary footage or dataset.
- `investigative` — established investigative outlet, OSINT group or accountability organization.
- `methodology` — tool, method, technical guide or research note.
- `data` — dataset, registry, casualty list, map, sanctions database or structured archive.
- `source_pointer` — useful lead source requiring independent verification before strong public claims.
- `monitoring` — useful for trend monitoring, not necessarily evidence.

## Ukrainian accountability priority

The Ukrainian layer must include Ukrainian investigative media, OSINT groups, civil-society accountability projects and data sources connected to:

- war-crimes documentation;
- Russian military identification;
- collaborators and occupation structures;
- Russian KIA/POW/MIA/missing/wounded;
- regional patterns of Russian losses and mobilization;
- Ukrainian map, dataset and verification work.

## Evocation.info

`https://evocation.info/` is included as a Ukrainian accountability source, but should be treated as `source_pointer` by default. It can be useful for leads about collaborators, propagandists, organizations and occupation-related networks, but claims about specific persons require independent verification before publication.

## Telegram rule

Telegram sources are not imported into public issues automatically. Telegram content must be evaluated for:

- original source status;
- repost chain;
- media authenticity;
- personal-data risk;
- accusation level;
- public-interest value;
- corroboration status.

## Publication standard

Before including a high-risk claim, especially about alleged war criminals, collaborators, POW/MIA/KIA identity or private persons, the issue should state clearly what is known, what is claimed, who claims it, and what remains unverified.
